Multipath protocols for public safety broadband resilience.

This project explores a Public Safety Broadband Network (PSBN) resilience testbed using Band 14, complementary access networks, and Multipath TCP (MPTCP) to study continuity across changing network conditions. The current beta focuses on platform setup, component integration, and early demonstrations of connectivity and situational awareness.

Abstract

A PSBN is a network dedicated to public safety operations and designed for first responders, including police, fire, and emergency medical services. It is built on modern LTE and 5G cellular standards. Our testbed investigates how Multipath TCP integration and multi-network access can support seamless handoff and continuity across 5G, LTE, and Wi-Fi in future public safety environments.

What is a PSBN?

A communications platform dedicated to public safety operations and designed around the needs of first responders.

Why this matters

Canada has dedicated Band 14 for PSBN, while interoperability challenges across agencies remain a major issue.

What makes this different

Our testbed combines MPTCP, multi-network handoff concepts, and TAK-based situational awareness into one platform.
