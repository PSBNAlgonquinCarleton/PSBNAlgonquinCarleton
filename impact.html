Real-World Impact
Why This Matters
Current emergency communication systems fail during critical moments due to network overload, lack of interoperability, and broken session continuity. Our PSBN prototype solves this by enabling seamless connectivity across WiFi, LTE, and 5G networks using Multipath TCP and modern 5G infrastructure.

Session Persistence

Applications remain active even when switching between networks. MPTCP ensures continuous data flow without reconnecting or losing state.

TAK Integration

Real-time situational awareness using TAK enables first responders to share live positions, messages, and data across the network.

Real Hardware Testbed

Built using SDR-based 5G infrastructure, routers, and antennas to simulate real-world public safety environments.

Live Demo

Demonstration of seamless handover between networks while maintaining active sessions.

Technical Impact
Our system demonstrates that session persistence can be achieved through a hybrid approach: network-layer roaming for voice and device-layer MPTCP for data. This aligns with real-world PSBN research goals and validates feasibility in a controlled testbed environment :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.
Future Potential
This prototype lays the foundation for nationwide deployment, enabling reliable communication for first responders, improved coordination between agencies, and advanced applications such as drones, IoT sensors, and real-time video streaming.
